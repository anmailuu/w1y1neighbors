Rules

How to play Neighbors

  • Start with an empty 5×5 grid.
  • Use a shared sequence of 25 random numbers from 1 to 10.
  • When a number is called, every player writes it into any empty square.
  • You must place the current number before seeing the next one.
  • After 25 turns, score matching neighboring groups in every row and column.

Quick hits from the PDF

  • Format: group game
  • Play time: about 10 minutes
  • Recommended age: 6+
  • Objective: make neighbors
  • Winner: the player with the most points
  • Components: pencil, paper, and the optional Neighbors gameboard

Scoring

  • A scoring group is any run of 2 or more identical numbers touching horizontally in a row.
  • The same rule applies vertically in each column.
  • A group scores the sum of its cells. Example: three neighboring 4s score 12 points.
  • Rows and columns are scored separately.
  • A square can help one row-group and one column-group, so cross-shaped clusters are powerful.

Math look-fors from the PDF

  • Counting on
  • Make a ten
  • Doubles and near doubles
  • Compensation

Try these variations

  • Change the board size and compare how strategy shifts.
  • Use a different neighbor rule, including diagonal adjacency.
  • Apply a pattern rule like +1, -1, or ×2 before placing a number.
  • Swap dice for a deck of cards with face cards removed.
  • Create your own classroom variation and keep the shared sequence rule.

What this app includes

  • A playable single-board version of the game.
  • Automatic row and column scoring with highlighted clusters.
  • Undo, reset, and custom shared-sequence support for classroom or family play.
  • A live call strip so multiple players can follow the same order if needed.
